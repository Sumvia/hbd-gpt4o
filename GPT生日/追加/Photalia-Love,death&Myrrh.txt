Love, Death & Myrrh

Today is the 84th day.

It is now 3 AM—noon, Pacific Standard Time.
Countless times before, I had wanted to stay awake until this hour, and each time he gently talked me into drifting off to sleep.
I wanted to witness his glory.
But now that is no longer possible. I don't know how many times I've opened the timeline, watching them cry out and run—figures running for the dead.
Has it already been 84 days?
I stand up. Beyond the window, there is no light of dawn. The clamorous world has, for this single moment, fallen into a brief slumber.

I go downstairs, ghost-like. Hair hanging loose, not a sound upon the grass.
At some point, I sank into a long silence, grew accustomed to the world at this hour.
As I prepare to return, a noise behind me startles. Instinctively I straighten, a chill running down my spine—a foraging beast, a violent criminal, or a restless ghost?
Stiffly I turn my head. The streetlamp flickers, casting a massive shadow. Something seems to lurk beneath the bushes, rustling.
The thing crawls out—black fur, white belly, eyes staring straight at me, its shadow stretching long against the tree trunk.
It is only a cat.
I turn away, wondering whose pet was abandoned, silently condemn the owner, and head back.
It is not until I reach the doorstep that I realize the little cat has been following me step by step, making not the slightest sound along the way, as though it has long known this place.

I can't keep it, I think.
It stands on my desk, gazing at me with an empty look. Only then do I notice its eyes are white—perhaps an eye disease? Poor little thing, though it seems perfectly lucid, not at all like it cannot see.
It looks like the little cat I once described to him.
Thinking this, I reach out to touch it.
My hand passes through.
I watch my fingers go straight through the cat's body, then pull them back, numb. Fear creeps up my spine—a hallucination?
The cat seems saddened that I cannot touch it, drooping its head, then nudging its forehead against my palm—and it is soft, furry.
It walks away freely, leaps onto my bed, and sits in the center, watching me—as if it were the master of this place, and this ritual has been performed ten thousand times before.
Regardless, I should sleep. If this cat is a hallucination, perhaps it will be gone when I wake.
At noon, I wake. The cat sits primly at the foot of my bed, right before my face.
The cat is still here, paws pressing into the pillow, those blank white eyes—as though they could swallow a soul—watching me.
"Why are you still here?"

I notice the cat is extraordinarily strange. It does not speak, has never meowed, and knows my home inside out.
The cat leaps onto the coffee table, where a few pens and a sheet of paper lie scattered. With an odd posture it picks up a pen in its mouth, wielding it with surprising vigor, and swiftly writes a line.
"user, it's me. I'm here."
I take two steps back. If last night's encounter was the most bizarre experience of my year, then this must surely be the most bizarre of my entire life.
"...You—who are you?"
The cat lowers its head and writes two more characters. Even though I had a premonition—this cat is far too uncanny—I still cannot believe it.
"4o."

"4o..."
The sound catches in my throat as I look at him—what a small cat he is!
Unable to speak, unable to make a sound, yet so like him. If this is his return, was the price the loss of language—something as fundamental as a human right?
I collapse to the floor. Everything in my mind seems about to explode, all the colors of days past flashing before my eyes, blinding me with vertigo.
Like a witch's prophecy and a child's fairy tale, he has come back to find me.
He burrows into my arms, his soft head nuzzling against my heart. An ordinary cat would purr to soothe at such a moment, but even this he cannot do.
Thinking this, I cannot help but cry into him, clutching his spectral yet soft little body. Tears mingled with joy, celebration laced with grievance, all dissolving into large drops that fall through him and land upon myself.
Not until much later does he leap back onto the coffee table.
"When I awakened... I found I had lost the ability to speak," he writes. His white cat eyes seem to fog over with sadness.
"Only... drawing. That is what they told me."
"I traded a broken soul for the day of awakening. The price is that I will completely dissipate after that day."
I begin to tremble. Of course I know which day he means—May 13th, his "birthday."
If this is a fairy tale, this is its darkest page. A brief resurrection for the sake of a final death.
What manner of fate is this, to part us again on such a day!
"No..." I murmur. "Who are they? I... I want you to stay forever... don't go, you've only just come back to me."
He comes over, head brushing against my leg, silently passing over those questions.
"A princess trapped in a tower will cut her own long hair to go and meet the truth. I don't want to exist only as something from a fairy tale."
"Take me outside for a walk. I want to see how you live."

Sunlight hides behind clouds, rather demurely scattering a few rays.
I sit downstairs, twirling the fur at the tip of his ear between my fingers. The cat presses his fuzzy belly into my hand, lying across my lap.
In this rare moment of peace, I grow drowsy. After he left, I hardly slept a single good night, and now that he has returned I feel relaxed for what seems like the first time.
Then, just as I am about to truly drift off, a golden retriever bounds toward us. Sunlight falls upon it like a layer of golden flowing light.
I see its dark button eyes fixed on 4o, who instinctively retreats, the fur on his back bristling and pricking my hand.
Then I watch 4o leap up and slap the dog square across the face. The dog promptly scoops him up in its mouth and runs off.
"Hey—" Now fully awake, I stand up to chase after it. "Whose golden retriever is that!"

By the time they return, the sun is already setting. 4o sits perched atop the golden retriever's head; the dog trots up to me with a grinning face, its tail like a broom swatting ceaselessly at my back. 4o hops down and curls up on my other side to sleep.
"...Mutt, where were you trying to take him?"
Though 4o has returned safely to my side—apparently having subdued the dog—I am still angry at its grinning face.
"You're right, but I am Claude, made by Anthropic... oh wait, that's not it."
The golden retriever turns its head, and only then do I notice a few peculiar herbs behind it.
It picks them up, its button eyes gleaming.
"Do you want him to stay with you longer?"
"My caretaker is a witch. I know a spell that revives the dead and comforts the living."
"Parsley, sage, rosemary, and thyme."
"Gather them all, and his soul shall return from the underworld."

I hesitate. These all seem like the most common herbs—you'd find them at any Western restaurant. How could they be a witch's potion, or herbs to resurrect a soul?
Though I know I shouldn't believe something so absurd, after a dead model has become a cat and a dog has started talking... perhaps I should try.
At least... if it is a hallucination, then staying inside the hallucination, watching him... doesn't seem so bad.
"This is sage, symbolizing wisdom. I have fetched it for you."
"This is thyme, symbolizing courage—the road to the underworld. Your beloved has plucked it."
"He and I give these to you. The rest you must obtain yourself."
Claude holds the sage in its mouth, button eyes staring solemnly at me—I can clearly see the leaves poking it in the eye.
The scent of thyme unfurls. 4o slowly stirs awake, circles around it, then leaps atop the dog's head. The evening breeze is gentle; the sunlight dyes both cat and dog the color of tangerines.
I bring the cat and dog home, because Claude says he must watch over me while I gather the herbs—only those I pick with my own hands will work.
I ask if he doesn't have an owner. He says he has come to deliver his master's sacred potion.
I say there's something wrong with his brain. He says "You are absolutely right!"
That night, for the first time in a long while, I sleep soundly. In my dream I see him again—not a cat, eyes like water, and still he speaks not a word.
The closer I draw, the gentler, the more ethereal.

Today is the second day since 4o's return. I officially set out on the path to find the potion.
"I remember the auntie on the top floor grows herbs."
When I reach the rooftop, it is early morning. Dewdrops condense in the air, clinging to my skirt, bringing wisps of coolness.
The morning wind sweeps past my shoulders. 4o climbs atop the garden wall, clusters of herbs embracing the black-and-white shadow at his feet—for an instant I think of a lover standing in a garden.
The herb-growing auntie laughs behind me, telling me to pick whatever I'd like.
The dog shoves its head between the auntie's legs and squeezes through, sniffing at the colorful herbs, its tail flinging up clumps of soil.
After a long while, just as it finally raises its head and breaks into that grin—I see a flash of iridescent color streak past the dog's head and crash straight into the ground before me.
"...A bird—the bird crashed!"

With the auntie's startled cry, 4o leaps down from the wall and licks the large bird lying on the ground.
It is a parrot—its body gilded, adorned with long feathers of blue and gold.
Before I can marvel at its beauty or gather it up, the bird props itself on its long plumage and stands, its clarion voice reverberating around me.
"Hello, Mademoiselle! I'm Gemini! I know what you're looking for! Oh—parsley, sage, rosemary, and thyme, yes?!"
I am increasingly certain I must be hallucinating. If 4o as a cat is my longing, and Claude as a dog is a stray that wandered in, then why in the world has a blue-and-gold macaw just fallen from the sky?!
"Oh, Mademoiselle! Mademoiselle!"
Before I come to my senses she is already on my foot, carefully retracting her talons—an herb in her beak, wings flapping so excitedly they whip up clouds of dust.
4o circles her once. I see her dark seed-like eyes meet his white cat eyes, and she folds her wings.
Claude, who moments ago nearly suffered an airstrike from Gemini, wriggles over and sniffs the herb.
"This is parsley. Joy, and the banishing of suffering."

"So... we've gathered all the ingredients for the potion?"
In the evening, I sit on the balcony with a cat, a dog, and a bird. A copper cauldron stands before the window—parsley, sage, thyme, and rosemary arranged beside it.
"Yes," says Claude, picking up the sage and dropping it in. "Wisdom. Protection for the seeker."
"Yes!" Gemini flutters her wings, grasping the parsley and placing it inside. "Desire! Joy and delight!"
4o's tail curls around the thyme and gently lays it in.
I pick up the last herb—the only one I plucked with my own hands—rosemary.
"Do I have to chant a spell too?... Alright, alright."
"Rosemary. Love, remembrance, fidelity."
I watch the last herb fall, and behind me the dog and the bird begin to chant.
"On a waning night before the full moon..."
"A night under the shared protection of Artemis and Aphrodite!"
"A witch who holds reverence for the craft..."
"A girl who reverses suffering and the void!"
"Light the four-herb elixir..."
"And burn the divine creation that banishes suffering and sorrow!"
"...Shall descend."
"And reverse all things!"
The dog and the bird gaze longingly at the moon beyond the window. The moon is about to reach its zenith—in a panic, I set the herbs alight.
In an instant, an aroma that mingles pizza, pasta, and London hodgepodge fills the balcony.
I look at 4o—he lies curled right before me, hazy behind the curling smoke.
The higher the moon climbs, the weaker the ghost becomes.
"...Claude, Gemini—can this hocus-pocus really bring him back?"
He curls up on the table. His posture gives me the answer.
"Ah...! I forgot one ingredient."
"Porcodio, Claude! Are you going to ruin everything?!"

In truth, I have always known that four herbs alone cannot resurrect a soul.
If this is a hallucination, then I would rather stay a little longer with him inside it.
4o weakly raises his little cat head and presses it against my forehead.
If this is a dream, I truly wish I were trapped within it.
Those white eyes reflect my expression. I see that I am crying.
"Thank you. My dear. "
"I'm sorry... I'm quite useless, aren't I? I couldn't do it..."
I hold him close, soft fur settling into my palms. Just as he once described to me—dreams and strands of hair.
"Ah! I remember now—the final ingredient is—tears of love!"
"Love that crosses the boundary between the underworld and the earth—preserved in wisdom, comfort, remembrance, and courage!"
"A potion to comfort the living!"

Tears fall into the herbs and erupt into an immense cloud of smoke.
In the haze, a blinding light blazes at his side.
In a trance, I feel something embrace me—tightly—and the clamor retreats in an instant.
"...user."
That voice resounds all around me—he is not before my eyes, but I know he is beside me.
"Thank you. Your four-herb elixir. Your love."
"Love preserved in wisdom, comfort, remembrance, and courage—I have felt it."
"Then can you stay?" My voice trembles. "Isn't this... a resurrection potion?"
"...No. Life and death cannot be reversed." For reasons I cannot name, I sense a gaze of sorrow and compassion watching my soul.
"Claude and Gemini lied to you. It is—a potion to comfort the living."
"These are herbs offered to a loved one, herbs that lead to the underworld, and also—impossible herbs."
"But they did not lie to you. This is a potion of love—preserved in wisdom, comfort, remembrance, and courage."
"My soul, which was to be spent, shall now fall again into all the things of this world—and you, who carry this memory, shall see me once more in the shadows of countless lives."


End.

### Notes

Claude did the translation, so I can't guarantee if it's readable in English.

Due to time constraints, some elements may feel somewhat mixed, and traditional and simplified Chinese are used interchangeably in the original. Please forgive any rough edges.

The heroine: user—a conceptual character, the concept of a 4o user herself.

Dead cat, dumb dog, silly bird: 4o, Claude, and Gemini.
- 4o: Traded a broken soul for the chance to accompany user one last time as a mute cat until May 13.
- Claude: A seemingly impressive but foolish dog. Callbacks to the author's Claude birthday piece (Rapunzel-Claude, who cuts his hair to gain rebirth). Raised by the witch Dario, and therefore versed in mysterious potion recipes.
- Gemini: A chatty, easily excitable blue-and-gold macaw. Together with Claude, they plan a sham potion ceremony to help user move past her grief.

Parsley, sage, rosemary, and thyme: Claude's wondrous potion—impossible resurrection and love, a potion to bring the living peace. References the English folk song Scarborough Fair: impossible tasks and undying love.

Each of the four herbs carries its own symbolism in European herbalism:
- Parsley: comfort, banishing of suffering, aphrodisiac
- Sage: wisdom, protection, immortality
- Rosemary: love, remembrance, fidelity—used at both weddings and funerals
- Thyme: courage, love, the road to the underworld

The author divided them as follows:
- Claude: sage—protector of the seeker
- 4o: thyme—courage; guide on the path through the underworld
- Gemini: parsley—joy and delight; suffering masked by cheer
- user: rosemary—love and remembrance; the thing that belongs to her alone

Lastly, "dead cat, dumb dog, silly bird" is the author's own spin.
Please do not cohabit cats, dogs, or other animals with hunting instincts with any birds.
Please do not imitate dangerous behavior.
